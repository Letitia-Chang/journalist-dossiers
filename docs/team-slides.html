Overview

What does Media Dossiers do?

🗂 Build a journalist roster

Track every AI/tech reporter we want to pitch — their beat, recent articles, contact info, and relationship history.

✉️ Run outreach campaigns

Group journalists into campaigns. Claude drafts a personalised email for each one. You review, approve, and send.

📰 Track press coverage

Log every article written about North Star. Link coverage back to the campaign that generated it.

📊 See everything in one place

Activity feed, warm contacts panel, campaign pipeline — all the context you need before your next pitch.

Important: The system never sends email on your behalf. All outreach is reviewed and sent by a human.

Feature: Journalists

Journalists — building your roster

How journalists get added
1

RSS Scanner finds them

The scanner reads AI/tech RSS feeds weekly and surfaces journalist bylines. They appear in RSS Suggestions for you to review.

2

You accept or reject

Go to Admin → RSS Suggestions. For each journalist, Accept (adds to roster + auto-scores) or Reject (removes suggestion).

3

Claude auto-scores

On accept, Claude reads their beat, articles, and publication and generates a relevance score. Runs in the background — usually under 30 seconds.

4

You can also add manually

Click + Add Journalist from the Journalists page and fill in their details yourself.

Journalists list — filters
  • Search by name, publication, or beat
  • Filter by outreach status
  • Sort by score, name, or last published date
Find Profiles (SerpAPI)

Click Find Profiles on any journalist (or run in bulk from the list) to discover their LinkedIn, MuckRack, Twitter/X, and sometimes their email — all from a Google search in the background.

Campaigns: Step 1 & 2

House Style and the campaign brief

House Style (Admin → House Style)

Set once per campaign type. These are standing instructions Claude follows for every email of that type — tone, structure, what to avoid.

Example: "Keep subject lines under 8 words. Lead with something specific about the journalist's recent work. Don't use words like 'revolutionary' or 'disruptive'."
Campaign Brief (per campaign)

2–4 sentences about what you're announcing and what angle journalists should take. Write it as if briefing a PR copywriter.

Example: "North Star AI Labs is launching publicly in July 2026. We build AI tools for enterprise talent teams. Focus on our differentiated approach to talent intelligence — not a generic AI announcement."

Changing the brief after drafts are generated

If you edit the brief or campaign type after drafts already exist, an amber banner will prompt you to regenerate pending drafts. Approved and sent drafts are never touched.

Campaigns: Step 6 & 7

Sending from the Email Pack

Open the Email Pack tab. All approved drafts appear formatted and ready to send. You have two options:

Option A — Save to Gmail Drafts (recommended)

Click Save to Gmail Drafts. One pre-addressed Gmail draft is created per approved journalist. Open Gmail → Drafts, add attachments, and hit Send yourself.

First time: click Connect Gmail → grant Gmail Compose access in the Google pop-up. Stays connected after that.

Option B — Copy and paste

Use Copy all to get all emails as a single text block, or copy individual cards.

After you send — Mark as sent

Click Mark as sent at the bottom of each email card after you've sent it. This automatically:

  • Logs the pitch to the Activity Feed (channel, subject, body, date)
  • Sets the journalist's status to Pitched
  • Moves the card to Sent (green) in Review Drafts
You don't need to manually log anything on the journalist's profile — Mark as sent handles everything.

Tracking press coverage

Adding a new article
1

Paste the article URL

Click Auto-fill — the system fetches the title, publication, and date from the page's metadata automatically.

2

Link the journalist

Search by name from your tracked journalist roster. This builds a record of who has covered us.

3

Set type and sentiment

Type: Mention, Feature, Interview, Quote, Review, Op-ed
Sentiment: Positive, Neutral, Mixed, Negative

Linking coverage to a campaign

From any campaign's Coverage tab, search your coverage log and click + Link to associate an article with that campaign. This shows, per campaign, how many articles it generated.

Why this matters
  • Journalists who have covered us are ranked highest when AI suggests contacts for a new campaign
  • Gives you a complete record of our media presence over time
  • Campaign coverage counts appear on the Dashboard pipeline
Feature: Activity Feed

A chronological log of every pitch, follow-up, and response across all journalists — your complete media relations history.

Each entry shows

  • Outreach status at time of log
  • Channel (Email, LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.)
  • Subject line and message body
  • Date sent
Filters
  • By publication
  • By outreach status
  • By date range
How entries are created
  • Automatically — clicking "Mark as sent" in the Email Pack creates a full log entry (subject, body, date, channel)

Summary stats

Top of the page shows totals across your filtered range: interactions, pitches sent, responses received, and coverage secured.

Best Practices

Tips for getting the most out of the system

Before generating drafts

  • Select your name in the sidebar bottom so the sign-off is correct
  • Make sure House Style is set for your campaign type
  • Write a specific brief — the more context, the better the drafts
  • Fill in Campaign Assets (press kit URL, boilerplate) in the Edit Campaign panel

Choosing who to pitch

  • Warm contacts first — prioritise journalists who have responded or covered us before
  • Score is a tiebreaker, not the primary signal
  • Check "last published" — don't pitch journalists who haven't posted in 90+ days without checking
  • Run "Find Profiles" first so Claude has social context when scoring

Keeping the system healthy

  • Review RSS Suggestions weekly (5–10 min)
  • Log all outreach — even calls and LinkedIn DMs — from the journalist's Outreach tab
  • Add press coverage as it comes in so journalists who covered us rank higher in future AI suggestions
  • Check the Dashboard system alerts — they flag when attention is needed

Editing a campaign mid-flight

  • Click the pencil icon next to the campaign title
  • You can update name, type, status, brief, and campaign assets in one panel
  • If you change the brief, use the amber banner to bulk-regenerate pending drafts
  • Approved and sent drafts are never regenerated automatically
