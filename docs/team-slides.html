Campaigns

Running a campaign — 6 steps

1
Write a brief — 2–4 sentences on what you're announcing and what angle you want journalists to take
2
Select journalists — click Suggest with AI and Claude ranks your roster by fit, warmth, and beat match
3
Claude writes a draft for each journalist — personalised to their beat, recent articles, and your brief
4
Review & approve — edit any draft inline, approve the ones you're happy with, skip the rest
5
Save to Gmail & send — one click creates a pre-addressed draft in Gmail; open it, add attachments, hit Send yourself
6
Mark as sent — logs to the Activity Feed, updates the journalist's status to Pitched. Done.
Campaigns — step 2

Picking the right journalists

Every journalist has a score out of 100 — a quick read on how good a fit they are for North Star's stories.

What's scoredMax
AI coverage depth25 pts
Startup / founder coverage20 pts
Fit for a company like ours20 pts
Publication reach & authority15 pts
Audience size10 pts
Has contact info10 pts
Score is a starting point — not the whole picture. A journalist who replied to a past pitch, or who has already covered us, is higher priority than a cold contact with a better score.

Suggest with AI

Click this in the Journalists tab and Claude reads your brief, then ranks every journalist in the roster. It prioritises:

  • Has covered North Star before — ranked first, every time
  • Warm relationship — responded to or in conversation with us
  • Beat matches the campaign angle — specific > generic
  • Has an email on file — can't pitch who you can't reach

Before generating drafts: make sure your name is selected at the bottom of the sidebar — Claude signs every email with the active user's name and title.

Campaigns — steps 4 & 5

Review, approve & send

In the Drafts tab

  • Edit — click into the subject or body and type directly
  • Approve — sends the draft to the Email Pack
  • Regenerate — give Claude new instructions and rewrite
  • Remove — exclude this journalist from the campaign

Draft statuses

ReadyGenerated — waiting for your review
ApprovedIn the Email Pack, ready to send
SentMarked as sent, logged to Activity Feed
FailedGeneration failed — write it manually in the editor

Email Pack tab

All approved drafts appear here. Click Save to Gmail Drafts — one pre-addressed draft is created in Gmail per journalist. Open Gmail, add attachments, hit Send yourself.

First time: click Connect Gmail and grant Compose access in the pop-up. Stays connected after that.

After you send — Mark as sent

Click Mark as sent on each email card. This automatically logs the pitch to the Activity Feed and sets the journalist's status to Pitched. No manual logging needed.

After the campaign

Tracking what happens next

📰

Press Coverage

When an article comes in, go to Press Coverage, paste the URL, and click Auto-fill — title, publication, and date are pulled automatically. Link the journalist and set the sentiment. Then open the campaign's Coverage tab and link the article to the campaign.

📋

Activity Feed

Every pitch, follow-up, and response in one chronological log. Entries are created automatically when you mark as sent — or add them manually for calls, LinkedIn DMs, or any off-system contact.

🌡️

Journalists with Covered, Responded, or In Conversation status appear in the Warm Contacts panel on the Dashboard and rank highest in AI suggestions for your next campaign.

Things to know
Select yourself before generating drafts
Claude signs every email with the active user's name. Pick your name at the bottom of the sidebar first.
Warm beats cold, every time
A journalist who has responded or covered us before is a better bet than a high-scoring stranger. The AI suggestion already weights this — trust it.
The brief is the most important input
The more specific your brief, the better the drafts. Write it as if briefing a copywriter — give the angle, not just the announcement.
